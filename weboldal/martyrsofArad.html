The Martyrs of Arad

Arad, Hungary - October 6, 1849 – Today, the land of Hungary mourns in its deepest sorrow as we lay to rest the brave souls who fought valiantly for our freedom, only to meet an unjust and tragic end. In the early hours of this fateful day, the Austrian authorities carried out the executions of thirteen Hungarian generals, the noble leaders who had led our valiant army in the struggle for independence. These men, whose names shall forever echo through the annals of our nation's history, are now immortalized as the Martyrs of Arad.

Their blood, spilled on the cold earth of this city, is a testament to their undying devotion to the cause of liberty. General Lázár Mészáros, General József Nagysándor, General Károly Károlyi, and their fellow commanders, many of whom were instrumental in the defense of our homeland, have been cruelly taken from us. These were men of honor, whose courage and unwavering belief in our nation's freedom knew no bounds. They led their troops with valor, offering their lives in the pursuit of a Hungary that was free from foreign rule.

As the sun rose today, casting its pale light upon the execution grounds, the nation was united in grief. The heart of Hungary, torn apart by this brutal and senseless act, weeps for the fallen heroes. The streets of Arad and every corner of our land are filled with the mournful cries of those who now carry the weight of this loss.

We, the people of Hungary, will forever remember these men as martyrs who gave their all for our liberty, and their sacrifice shall never be forgotten. Each name, etched in our hearts, will serve as a beacon of hope for future generations, reminding us of the high cost of freedom.

Let us not let their deaths be in vain. Let us stand together, with unyielding resolve, to honor their memory by continuing the fight for the independence they so dearly sought. Though the revolution may have been quelled, their spirits live on in the hearts of every Hungarian who dares to dream of a free and sovereign nation.

May God rest their souls, and may Hungary rise again to claim its rightful place among the free nations of the world.
