Sándor Petőfi

The Poet of the Hungarian Revolution

Sándor Petőfi (1823-1849) was one of Hungary's greatest poets, a central figure of Hungarian literature, and a passionate supporter of the 1848 Hungarian Revolution. Petőfi’s poetry reflected the national struggles and aspirations of the Hungarian people, and he became a symbol of freedom and independence.

His most famous poem, *Nemzeti dal* (National Song), was read out loud on March 15, 1848, in Pest, marking the beginning of the revolution. The poem called for the Hungarian people to rise up and demand their rights, and it quickly became the rallying cry for revolutionaries. Petőfi’s words captured the hearts of Hungarians and inspired a nationwide movement for freedom and reform.

During the revolution, Petőfi enlisted in the Hungarian army to fight for independence. Unfortunately, he disappeared on the battlefield during the Battle of Segesvár in 1849. His death remains a mystery, and he is often remembered as a martyr of the Hungarian struggle for independence.

Despite his early death at the age of 26, Sándor Petőfi's literary legacy endures in Hungary and beyond. His works have inspired countless generations to fight for justice, freedom, and national pride. Petőfi’s poetry continues to be celebrated as one of the most important contributions to Hungarian culture and history.
